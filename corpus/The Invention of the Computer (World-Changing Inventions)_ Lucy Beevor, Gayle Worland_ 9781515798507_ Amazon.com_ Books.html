5.0 out of 5 stars Good book for the young researcher
April 17, 2019
Format: PaperbackVerified Purchase
My second grade son was doing a project on computers as his first foray into learning how to conduct research. We worked together both on internet searches and going to the library. The trouble with searching the internet is that so many of the sources you find there (i.e. Wikipedia) tend to be written on a higher reading level than the average 2nd or 3rd grader. While the library had some good sources in the children's section, we found that many of them were somewhat old. We ended up buying this book from Amazon, and then plan on gifting it to his school library. As for the book, it is written in language that doesn't "talk down" to the young reader. It covers a nice chunk of information about each stage in computer evolution without being too overwhelming. For developing early researching skills, this book, as I'm sure the other topics in this series, help to present a orderly representation of the progress of the product's design. I particularly liked the timeline at the back for this. Highly recommended for young researchers ages 7 - 10.
